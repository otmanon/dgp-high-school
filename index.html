DGP Academy


About

DGP Academy is a week-long outreach event hosted by the esteemed Dynamic Graphics Project (DGP) laboratory at the University of Toronto's Computer Science Department. Tailored for high school students aspiring to understand the nuances of academia, this event offers a focused exploration of computer graphics research, featuring hands-on lab activities, insightful lectures, and a comprehensive view of graduate school life. Throughout the week, participants will actively participate in lab sessions, gaining practical experience and collaborating with peers and experienced researchers. Participants will work on week-long projects, whose scope span physics based animation, geometry processing, computational fabrication, and more ! DGP Academy is crafted to provide a substantive understanding of cutting-edge graphics research, emphasizing the disciplined and methodical approach required in academic pursuits. In addition to technical immersion, attendees will receive guidance on the academic journey, demystifying the graduate school experience and shedding light on the expectations and challenges of pursuing higher education.
